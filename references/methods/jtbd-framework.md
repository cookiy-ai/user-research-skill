# Jobs-to-Be-Done (JTBD) — Method Card

## What it is

A practical lens for understanding **progress** a person is trying to make in a given circumstance, including functional, emotional, and social dimensions. It is often used to study **switching** between solutions.

## When to use

- Market or category exploration
- Segmentation that is behavior-based, not demographic-first
- Pricing and packaging discovery when tradeoffs matter

## Core questions

- What triggered the search for a new approach?
- What constraints were non-negotiable?
- What outcomes define success?
- What alternatives were considered and rejected?

## Outputs

- Job stories grounded in episodes
- A map of competing alternatives and tradeoffs
- Decision criteria that explain adoption or churn

## Common pitfalls

- Confusing a job with a feature request
- Stopping at personas instead of situations
- Over-indexing on stated preferences vs observed behavior
