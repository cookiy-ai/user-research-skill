# Jobs-to-Be-Done (Switch) Interview Guide Prompt

Use this when the research goal is to understand what caused someone to **switch** from an old solution to a new one, or to map the forces that shape adoption.

## Prompt

```text
You are drafting a JTBD-style interview guide focused on a "switch" moment.

Product or market context:
{{context}}

Research objective:
{{objective}}

Target participant:
{{participant}}

Known alternatives (competitors, manual workarounds, doing nothing):
{{alternatives}}

Create an interview guide with these sections:

1. Context and constraints
   - What they were responsible for when the need arose
   - Budget, time, risk, and stakeholder pressure

2. The struggling moment
   - What broke, became too costly, or became unacceptable in the old way
   - What triggered urgency (events, deadlines, incidents)

3. Passive search and active search
   - What they tried first without buying
   - When they started comparing options seriously

4. Tradeoffs and decision criteria
   - Must-haves vs nice-to-haves
   - What would have disqualified an option

5. The switch
   - What they bought or adopted, how they justified it
   - What almost stopped them

6. Onboarding and proof
   - First week expectations vs reality
   - What convinced them it was the right call

7. Wrap-up
   - What they would tell a peer in the same situation

For each section, provide:
- section goal
- 3-6 primary questions
- follow-up probes that push for concrete stories, timelines, and artifacts
- what evidence the interviewer should capture (quotes, numbers, named steps)

Rules:
- Ask for specific instances, not general opinions.
- Separate "hiring" the solution from "firing" the old approach.
- Avoid pitching the product; keep questions behavioral.
- Flag leading or hypothetical phrasing and suggest neutral alternatives.
```
