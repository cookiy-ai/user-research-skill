# Example Readout: Mobile Checkout Friction

## Research Overview

- Objective: understand why mobile shoppers abandon checkout before payment
- Inputs: 6 interview summaries, 38 support tickets, funnel drop-off notes

## Top Findings

### 1. Shipping-cost surprise is the strongest abandonment trigger

- Why it matters: users are willing to continue until the final price changes the value calculation
- Evidence: 4 of 6 interview participants explicitly said the final total changed their intent; support tickets repeatedly mention "hidden shipping"
- Confidence: High

### 2. Account creation feels like work at the wrong moment

- Why it matters: users are ready to buy, not to set up a relationship
- Evidence: repeated frustration around password creation and email verification during mobile checkout
- Confidence: Medium

### 3. Mobile coupon handling creates mistrust

- Why it matters: users are not just failing to redeem a discount, they are losing confidence that the checkout is functioning correctly
- Evidence: participants described repeated code-entry attempts and uncertainty about whether the discount applied
- Confidence: Medium

## Immediate Actions

- Surface shipping estimates earlier
- Test guest checkout for first purchase
- Simplify coupon application feedback on mobile

## Open Questions

- How much abandonment would be reduced by earlier price transparency alone?
- Are guest checkout issues equally severe for returning users?
